If you optimize the next 90 days against this protocol, the most likely outcome is: Vitamin D corrects into optimal range, triglycerides drop below 100, estrogen normalizes, testosterone rises on its own without intervention, TSH either normalizes or gets a clear diagnosis, and homocysteine continues its downward trajectory. That is what peak health at thirty-two looks like for you specifically.

§04

Genetic blueprint

The variants that actually affect your day-to-day health choices. Omitted: pharmacogenomic variants for drugs you're not taking, and variants with minor or no clinical consequence.

MTHFR C677T Heterozygous
Folate conversion to active methylfolate.

Reduced MTHFR enzyme activity. One of the most studied and actionable variants in consumer genomics. Affects methylation, which touches cardiovascular risk, mood regulation, gut lining integrity, and detoxification.

Remedy: Methylated B-complex with 5-MTHF (not folic acid). Keep homocysteine below 7. Avoid fortified foods with synthetic folic acid where practical.
MTRR I22M (A66G) Homozygous
B12 recycling into its active form.

Two copies — compounds the MTHFR variant on the same methylation pathway. Associated with elevated homocysteine risk and, in some studies, higher risk of neural tube defects in offspring (relevant for family planning).

Remedy: Methylcobalamin (active B12), not cyanocobalamin. Thorne Methyl-Guard Plus covers this. Target serum B12 above 600 pg/mL.
CFH Y402H (rs1061170) Heterozygous
Complement factor H — immune/inflammation regulation in the eye.

Pathogenic risk factor for age-related macular degeneration. Won't matter for decades, but interventions are most effective when started early. Heterozygotes have meaningfully elevated lifetime AMD risk vs non-carriers.

Remedy: Annual dilated eye exams. UV-blocking sunglasses. Never smoke. Consider AREDS2 formulation (lutein, zeaxanthin, omega-3, zinc). Fish oil you're already taking overlaps.
PNPLA3 Heterozygous
Liver fat metabolism.

Increases susceptibility to non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) and amplifies alcohol's liver impact. Context matters: you've had three separate episodes of elevated AST/ALT on record. This gene is part of why.

Remedy: Sustained alcohol minimization (not just through June). Low refined carb intake. Periodic liver enzyme monitoring (ALT/AST) at annual physicals. Omega-3s help.
COMT Val158Met Heterozygous
Dopamine clearance in the prefrontal cortex.

The "warrior-worrier" gene. Met allele slows dopamine breakdown — higher baseline dopamine, stronger stress reactivity, potentially better working memory under low stress. Directly relevant to your anxiety diagnosis.

Remedy: Manage stimulants (caffeine, nicotine) more tightly than average. Exercise — especially the Muay Thai — is the most powerful natural dopamine regulator. Sleep quality matters disproportionately. Magnesium glycinate evening dose can help.
AMPD1 Carrier
Muscle AMP deaminase — energy/ATP pathway in muscle.

Carrier status. Most carriers are asymptomatic, but some experience disproportionate post-exercise muscle fatigue or slower recovery. Worth knowing given Muay Thai twice weekly.

Remedy: Prioritize post-training protein + carbs within 60 minutes. Consider magnesium supplementation (supports ATP). D-ribose can help if recovery becomes an issue. Monitor how you feel post-session.
ADD1 G460W Heterozygous
Sodium-sensitive blood pressure regulation.

Salt-sensitive hypertension risk variant. Means a low-sodium diet is unusually effective for you specifically, and high-sodium days will push your BP up more than they would for others.

Remedy: DASH-style eating. Limit processed foods (the main sodium source). Potassium-rich foods help counteract (leafy greens, avocado, potatoes). Target <2,300mg sodium/day, <1,500mg if BP stays elevated.
VKORC1 Heterozygous
Warfarin metabolism.

Matters only if you're ever prescribed blood thinners — your standard dose will differ. Flag this to any prescribing physician for life. Not a daily concern.

Remedy: Note in your medical file. Mention if anticoagulation is ever discussed.
NAT2 (slow acetylator) Homozygous
Drug/toxin metabolism.

You metabolize certain drugs (isoniazid, sulfonamides, some chemotherapy) more slowly than average. Also associated with slightly elevated sensitivity to certain dietary carcinogens (well-charred meats).

Remedy: Avoid extremely well-done/charred meats as a habit. Mention slow acetylator status to any prescribing physician. Not a daily-life constraint otherwise.
LCT / MCM6 Homozygous AA (good)
Lactase persistence.

Two copies of the lactase persistence allele. You can digest dairy fully throughout life. No genetic basis for avoiding dairy.

Remedy: None needed. Enjoy.

§07

Diet protocol

Your dietary picture is dominated by two imperatives: lower triglycerides and reduce liver stress. Both respond to the same pattern — less refined carbohydrate, less alcohol, more fiber, more omega-3s, more protein. Your ADD1 variant adds salt-sensitivity as a third lever.

Emphasize

  • Fatty cold-water fish Salmon, sardines, mackerel, trout — 2–3x weekly. Directly lowers triglycerides and supports your CFH variant.
  • Leafy greens & cruciferous Spinach, kale, broccoli, Brussels sprouts. Natural folate (the food form your MTHFR prefers), plus estrogen-clearance support via DIM/indole-3-carbinol.
  • Lean protein at every meal 40g+ per meal. Grass-fed beef, wild game, chicken thighs, eggs. Supports free testosterone, blunts glucose response, satiates.
  • Healthy fats Extra virgin olive oil, avocado, walnuts, almonds. Essential for D3 absorption and satiety. Anti-inflammatory profile.
  • Berries & low-glycemic fruit Blueberries, raspberries, green apples, kiwi. Fiber + antioxidants + low trig impact.
  • Fermented foods Sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, plain Greek yogurt. Directly supports the gut repair protocol.
  • Potassium-rich foods Avocado, potatoes, beans, bananas. Counter-balances sodium for your ADD1 variant.

Limit or avoid

  • Alcohol Non-negotiable through June 11 and strongly recommended ongoing. PNPLA3 + history of elevated AST/ALT = your liver takes disproportionate damage.
  • Refined carbs & added sugar White bread, pastries, sodas, sweetened coffee drinks. These are the single biggest trigger for your triglyceride elevation.
  • Folic acid (synthetic) Fortified cereals, bargain multivitamins, enriched flour products. Competes with methylfolate at receptors in MTHFR carriers.
  • Industrial seed oils Soybean, corn, cottonseed oils — common in fried and processed foods. Pro-inflammatory omega-6 load works against your EPA protocol.
  • High-sodium processed foods Deli meat, packaged snacks, frozen meals, fast food. ADD1 variant makes your BP more reactive.
  • Charred / well-done meats Slow acetylator genotype (NAT2) = slower clearance of certain dietary carcinogens. Grill, don't incinerate.
  • Gluten (trial period) Per PCP's elimination suggestion for chronic diarrhea. 4-week strict elimination, then reintroduce and observe. Informs the GI picture.

§09

Lifestyle pillars

The non-supplement, non-exercise inputs. These are what actually produce long-term change when done consistently.

I.

Sleep

Target 7.5–8h in bed, 10pm–6am ideally. Dark, cool room. Phone out of bedroom after 9pm — environmental design over willpower. Your chronic insomnia diagnosis makes this foundational, not optional. Magnesium glycinate supports this.

II.

Sunlight

10–20 minutes within an hour of waking, no sunglasses, outdoors. Separately, 10 min midday if you can. Direct effect on circadian regulation, mood, and vitamin D synthesis. Austin sun + Texas latitude make this genuinely accessible year-round.

III.

Stress regulation

Your COMT variant means stress hits harder. Muay Thai is excellent for dopamine regulation. Beyond that: breathwork (box breathing pre-cold calls), journaling, Friday dinner tradition, time outdoors. Anxiety management is a genetic requirement, not a weakness.

IV.

Alcohol

The single most leveraged variable on this entire document. Every biomarker flagged — liver, triglycerides, estrogen, BP, weight, sleep, anxiety — improves when alcohol is out. Your PNPLA3 variant makes your body pay a premium for every drink. The bet through June 11 is protective; continuing the pattern is transformative.

V.

Hydration

80–100oz water daily. A pinch of sea salt in your first glass supports adrenal function and electrolytes (coffee and Muay Thai both deplete). Track for a week to baseline.

VI.

Environmental toxin load

Per your PCP's specific advice: check products through EWG's Skin Deep database. Swap to fragrance-free detergent, mineral (zinc oxide) sunscreen, stainless/glass cookware. Not urgent, not optional long-term — and family history of lung cancer adds weight.

VII.

Sauna + cold

If/when accessible. Sauna 3–4x/week (15–20 min 170°F) improves cardiovascular markers and lowers all-cause mortality in the longest longitudinal studies. Cold exposure (cold shower finish) supports dopamine regulation and metabolic health.

VIII.

Connection

Friday dinner with the 17-year friends, family, faith community exploration — these aren't soft. Social connection quality predicts all-cause mortality more strongly than most clinical interventions. Keep showing up.
