The big picture

Three overlapping threads run through your entire health record: metabolic stress (lipids, liver, emerging insulin resistance), gut dysfunction (chronic diarrhea unresolved for a decade), and mental health under load (anxiety, sleep, and the alcohol coping loop that worsens the first two). Your genetics — particularly the MTHFR/MTRR methylation variants and the PNPLA3 liver variant — amplify each of these threads rather than cause them.

The clean read of your data is that you are not broken. You are tunable. Every single one of your flagged biomarkers is directionally correctable with consistent inputs over 60–90 days. You already have the hardest piece in place: the behavioral infrastructure — sobriety, Muay Thai, a morning routine, a daily regiment tracker. The remaining work is tightening the supplement protocol, getting the thyroid answer, and making consistency non-negotiable on the few things that have historically been start-stop — namely Vitamin D.

If you optimize the next 90 days against this protocol, the most likely outcome is: Vitamin D corrects into optimal range, triglycerides drop below 100, estrogen normalizes, testosterone rises on its own without intervention, TSH either normalizes or gets a clear diagnosis, and homocysteine continues its downward trajectory. That is what peak health at thirty-two looks like for you specifically.

Genetic blueprint

The variants that actually affect your day-to-day health choices. Omitted: pharmacogenomic variants for drugs you're not taking, and variants with minor or no clinical consequence.

MTHFR C677T Heterozygous
Folate conversion to active methylfolate.

Reduced MTHFR enzyme activity. Affects methylation, which touches cardiovascular risk, mood regulation, gut lining integrity, and detoxification.

Remedy: Methylated B-complex with 5-MTHF (not folic acid). Keep homocysteine below 7. Avoid fortified foods with synthetic folic acid where practical.
MTRR I22M (A66G) Homozygous
B12 recycling into its active form.

Two copies — compounds the MTHFR variant on the same methylation pathway. Associated with elevated homocysteine risk and, in some studies, higher risk of neural tube defects in offspring.

Remedy: Methylcobalamin (active B12), not cyanocobalamin. Thorne Methyl-Guard Plus covers this. Target serum B12 above 600 pg/mL.
CFH Y402H Heterozygous
Complement factor H — eye immune/inflammation regulation.

Pathogenic risk factor for age-related macular degeneration. Won't matter for decades, but interventions are most effective when started early.

Remedy: Annual dilated eye exams. UV-blocking sunglasses. Never smoke. Consider AREDS2 formulation (lutein, zeaxanthin, omega-3, zinc).
PNPLA3 Heterozygous
Liver fat metabolism.

Increases susceptibility to non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD) and amplifies alcohol's liver impact. You've had three separate episodes of elevated AST/ALT on record. This gene is part of why.

Remedy: Sustained alcohol minimization. Low refined carb intake. Periodic liver enzyme monitoring at annual physicals. Omega-3s help.
COMT Val158Met Heterozygous
Dopamine clearance in the prefrontal cortex.

The "warrior-worrier" gene. Met allele slows dopamine breakdown — higher baseline dopamine, stronger stress reactivity. Directly relevant to your anxiety diagnosis.

Remedy: Manage stimulants (caffeine hard-cutoff before 11am). Muay Thai regulates dopamine. Sleep quality matters disproportionately. Magnesium glycinate evening dose helps.
AMPD1 Carrier
Muscle ATP pathway.

Carrier status. Most carriers are asymptomatic, but some experience disproportionate post-exercise muscle fatigue or slower recovery.

Remedy: Post-training protein + carbs within 60 minutes. Magnesium supplementation. Creatine if recovery lags.
ADD1 G460W Heterozygous
Sodium-sensitive BP regulation.

Salt-sensitive hypertension risk variant. Means a low-sodium diet is unusually effective for you specifically.

Remedy: DASH-style eating. Limit processed foods. Potassium-rich foods (leafy greens, avocado, potatoes). Target <2,300mg sodium/day.
VKORC1 Heterozygous
Warfarin metabolism.

Matters only if you're ever prescribed blood thinners. Flag to any prescribing physician for life.

Remedy: Note in medical file. Not a daily concern.
NAT2 (slow acetylator) Homozygous
Drug/toxin metabolism.

Slower metabolism of certain drugs and dietary carcinogens (charred meats).

Remedy: Avoid extremely well-done/charred meats as a habit. Mention slow acetylator status to any prescribing physician.
LCT / MCM6 Homozygous AA
Lactase persistence.

Two copies of lactase persistence allele. You digest dairy fully throughout life.

Remedy: None needed. Enjoy.

Diet protocol

Your dietary picture is dominated by two imperatives: lower triglycerides and reduce liver stress. Both respond to the same pattern — less refined carbohydrate, less alcohol, more fiber, more omega-3s, more protein. Your ADD1 variant adds salt-sensitivity as a third lever.

Emphasize

  • Fatty cold-water fishSalmon, sardines, mackerel, trout — 2–3x weekly. Directly lowers triglycerides, supports CFH variant.
  • Leafy greens & cruciferousSpinach, kale, broccoli, Brussels sprouts. Natural folate + DIM/indole-3-carbinol for estrogen clearance.
  • Lean protein at every meal40g+ per meal. Grass-fed beef, wild game, chicken thighs, eggs.
  • Healthy fatsExtra virgin olive oil, avocado, walnuts, almonds. Essential for D3 absorption.
  • Berries & low-glycemic fruitBlueberries, raspberries, green apples, kiwi. Fiber + antioxidants.
  • Fermented foodsSauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, plain Greek yogurt. Supports gut repair protocol.
  • Potassium-rich foodsAvocado, potatoes, beans, bananas. Counters sodium for ADD1 variant.

Limit or avoid

  • AlcoholNon-negotiable through June 11 and strongly recommended ongoing. PNPLA3 + history of elevated AST/ALT.
  • Refined carbs & added sugarWhite bread, pastries, sodas. Biggest trigger for triglyceride elevation.
  • Folic acid (synthetic)Fortified cereals, cheap multivitamins. Competes with methylfolate in MTHFR carriers.
  • Industrial seed oilsSoybean, corn, cottonseed. Pro-inflammatory omega-6 load.
  • High-sodium processed foodsDeli meat, packaged snacks, fast food. ADD1 makes BP more reactive.

  • Gluten (trial period)Per PCP's elimination suggestion. 4-week strict trial, then reintroduce and observe.

Hard caffeine cutoff. Not 2pm — your COMT variant processes caffeine slower. Afternoon coffee is still active at 9pm.
At sunset

2 hrs before bed
Dim lighting. Lamps, not overheads. Blue-light filtering on screens. Caloric intake ends here — late eating spikes triglycerides.
30 min before bed
Magnesium glycinate 200–400mg. No screens. Reading, journaling, conversation. Phone charges outside the bedroom.
Overnight
Cool (65–68°F), dark, quiet. Blackout curtains. White noise if needed. Temperature drop is the most underrated sleep lever.

Fixing sleep isn't one intervention among many; it's an upstream multiplier.
